1. SYNOPSIS

Study Title:        PHOENIX-LUNG 3
Phase:              Phase 3
Study Design:       Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, multicenter
Indication:         First-line treatment of locally advanced or metastatic NSCLC
                    with PD-L1 TPS ≥ 1%
Study Population:   Adults ≥ 18 years with histologically confirmed NSCLC
Target Enrollment:  680 patients (340 per arm)
Number of Sites:    ~120 sites across North America, Europe, and Asia-Pacific

Treatment Arms:
  Arm A (Experimental): Nixatinib 200 mg PO BID + Pembrolizumab 200 mg IV Q3W
  Arm B (Control):      Placebo PO BID + Pembrolizumab 200 mg IV Q3W

Primary Endpoint:   Progression-Free Survival (PFS) per RECIST v1.1 by BICR
Key Secondary:      Overall Survival (OS), Objective Response Rate (ORR),
                    Duration of Response (DOR), Patient-Reported Outcomes (PROs)

Study Duration:     Approximately 48 months (24 months enrollment + 24 months follow-up)
Treatment Duration: Until disease progression, unacceptable toxicity, or max 35 cycles
                    of pembrolizumab (approximately 2 years)

2. INTRODUCTION AND BACKGROUND

2.1 Disease Background

Non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) accounts for approximately 85% of all lung
cancers and remains the leading cause of cancer-related mortality worldwide. Despite
advances in immunotherapy, particularly with PD-1/PD-L1 checkpoint inhibitors,
many patients develop primary or acquired resistance. The 5-year overall survival
rate for metastatic NSCLC remains approximately 7-8%.

2.2 Investigational Product: Nixatinib (NIX-4821)

Nixatinib is a novel, orally bioavailable, selective inhibitor of the KRAS G12C
mutation found in approximately 13% of NSCLC cases. In preclinical models, nixatinib
demonstrated synergistic antitumor activity when combined with anti-PD-1 antibodies,
enhancing T-cell infiltration into the tumor microenvironment.

Mechanism of Action:
- Direct covalent binding to KRAS G12C in the GDP-bound (inactive) state
- Inhibition of RAF-MEK-ERK signaling pathway
- Enhanced MHC class I antigen presentation on tumor cells
- Increased CD8+ T-cell tumor infiltration

2.3 Preclinical Data

In KRAS G12C-mutant NSCLC xenograft models:
- Nixatinib monotherapy: 68% tumor growth inhibition (TGI)
- Anti-PD-1 monotherapy: 42% TGI
- Combination: 94% TGI with 30% complete response rate

2.4 Phase 1/2 Data (Study NIX-ONC-2024-001)

Dose-escalation (Phase 1, n=48):
- MTD: 250 mg BID (RP2D: 200 mg BID based on PK/PD modeling)
- DLTs at 300 mg BID: Grade 3 hepatotoxicity (2/6), Grade 3 rash (1/6)

Dose-expansion (Phase 2, n=82):
- ORR: 52% (95% CI: 41-63%)
- DCR: 84% (95% CI: 75-91%)
- Median PFS: 11.2 months (95% CI: 8.4-14.1)
- Grade ≥3 AEs: 34% (most common: ALT/AST elevation, rash, diarrhea)

2.5 Rationale for the Study

The combination of nixatinib with pembrolizumab is hypothesized to overcome
resistance to PD-1 monotherapy by:
1. Enhancing tumor antigen presentation (KRAS G12C inhibition)
2. Increasing T-cell recognition and killing (dual pathway blockade)
3. Modifying the immunosuppressive tumor microenvironment

This Phase 3 study will determine whether the combination provides clinically
meaningful improvement in PFS compared to pembrolizumab alone in the first-line
setting for PD-L1-positive NSCLC patients harboring KRAS G12C mutations.

3. STUDY OBJECTIVES AND ENDPOINTS

3.1 Primary Objective

To compare PFS of nixatinib + pembrolizumab versus placebo + pembrolizumab in
patients with previously untreated, PD-L1 positive (TPS ≥ 1%), locally advanced
or metastatic NSCLC harboring KRAS G12C mutation.

Primary Endpoint: PFS as assessed by BICR per RECIST v1.1

3.2 Key Secondary Objectives and Endpoints

  Objective                                    Endpoint
  ─────────────────────────────────────────    ──────────────────────────────
  Compare OS between treatment arms            Overall survival
  Compare ORR between treatment arms           Objective response rate (BICR)
  Evaluate DOR                                 Duration of response (BICR)
  Assess patient-reported outcomes             EORTC QLQ-C30, QLQ-LC13
  Evaluate safety and tolerability             AEs, SAEs per CTCAE v5.0

3.3 Exploratory Objectives

- Evaluate PFS and ORR in PD-L1 TPS ≥ 50% subgroup
- Characterize ctDNA dynamics as early predictor of response
- Evaluate tumor mutational burden (TMB) as predictive biomarker
- Assess pharmacokinetics of nixatinib in combination setting
- Evaluate immune cell infiltration in paired biopsies (optional)

4. STUDY DESIGN

4.1 Overall Design

This is a Phase 3, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, multicenter,
international study. Approximately 680 patients will be randomized 1:1 to receive
either nixatinib + pembrolizumab or placebo + pembrolizumab.

Randomization will be stratified by:
- PD-L1 TPS (≥ 50% vs. 1-49%)
- ECOG PS (0 vs. 1)
- Geographic region (North America vs. Europe vs. Asia-Pacific)

4.2 Study Periods

Screening:    Up to 28 days prior to randomization
Treatment:    Until PD, unacceptable toxicity, or 35 cycles pembrolizumab
Follow-up:    Every 12 weeks until death or study closure
Survival:     Every 12 weeks after treatment discontinuation

4.3 Dose Modifications

Nixatinib/Placebo dose reductions:
  Level 0 (starting): 200 mg BID
  Level -1:           150 mg BID
  Level -2:           100 mg BID
  Level -3:           Permanent discontinuation

Pembrolizumab:
  No dose reductions permitted per prescribing information.
  Dosing delays up to 12 weeks allowed for immune-related AEs.

4.4 Crossover

No crossover from the control arm to the experimental arm will be permitted
during the primary analysis period. After the primary PFS analysis, patients
in the control arm who have documented PD may be offered open-label nixatinib
access through a separate extension study.

5. STUDY POPULATION

5.1 Inclusion Criteria

  1. Age ≥ 18 years at time of informed consent
  2. Histologically or cytologically confirmed NSCLC (non-squamous or squamous)
  3. Locally advanced (Stage IIIB/IIIC not amenable to curative therapy) or
     metastatic (Stage IV) disease per AJCC 8th edition
  4. Documented KRAS G12C mutation by central laboratory testing (tissue or ctDNA)
  5. PD-L1 TPS ≥ 1% by IHC (22C3 pharmDx assay, central lab)
  6. No prior systemic therapy for advanced/metastatic NSCLC
     (Prior adjuvant/neoadjuvant therapy completed ≥ 12 months prior allowed)
  7. Measurable disease per RECIST v1.1
  8. ECOG Performance Status 0 or 1
  9. Adequate organ function:
     a. Absolute neutrophil count ≥ 1,500/µL
     b. Platelets ≥ 100,000/µL
     c. Hemoglobin ≥ 9.0 g/dL
     d. Serum creatinine ≤ 1.5× ULN or CrCl ≥ 50 mL/min (Cockcroft-Gault)
     e. Total bilirubin ≤ 1.5× ULN (≤ 3× ULN if Gilbert's syndrome)
     f. AST/ALT ≤ 2.5× ULN (≤ 5× ULN if liver metastases)
     g. INR/PT and aPTT ≤ 1.5× ULN
  10. Women of childbearing potential: negative serum pregnancy test and
      agreement to use effective contraception during study and for 120 days
      after last dose
  11. Written informed consent

5.2 Exclusion Criteria

  1. Known EGFR, ALK, ROS1, BRAF V600E, MET exon 14, RET, or NTRK alterations
  2. Symptomatic brain metastases (treated, stable brain mets allowed if
     off steroids for ≥ 14 days and no neurological symptoms)
  3. Active autoimmune disease requiring systemic immunosuppression
  4. Known interstitial lung disease or history of pneumonitis requiring steroids
  5. Active infection requiring systemic antimicrobial therapy
  6. Known HIV, active HBV, or active HCV infection
  7. Prior treatment with KRAS G12C inhibitor
  8. Prior treatment with anti-PD-1, anti-PD-L1, or anti-CTLA-4 antibody
  9. Known hypersensitivity to nixatinib, pembrolizumab, or excipients
  10. Concurrent use of strong CYP3A4 inhibitors or inducers
  11. QTcF > 470 ms at screening
  12. Major surgery within 4 weeks of randomization
  13. Active second malignancy (exceptions: non-melanoma skin cancer,
      carcinoma in situ, superficial bladder cancer)
  14. Pregnancy or breastfeeding

6. TREATMENT PLAN

6.1 Investigational Medicinal Product

Drug:               Nixatinib (NIX-4821)
Formulation:        Film-coated tablet, 100 mg
Dose:               200 mg (2 × 100 mg tablets) BID
Route:              Oral
Schedule:           Continuous daily dosing (Days 1-21 of each 21-day cycle)
Food Effect:        Administer with or without food; consistent timing recommended

6.2 Comparator

Drug:               Matching placebo
Formulation:        Film-coated tablet, identical appearance to nixatinib
Dose:               2 tablets BID
Route:              Oral
Schedule:           Continuous daily dosing

6.3 Background Therapy

Drug:               Pembrolizumab
Dose:               200 mg IV
Schedule:           Day 1 of each 21-day cycle
Duration:           Maximum 35 cycles (approximately 2 years)
Premedication:      Per institutional guidelines

6.4 Concomitant Medications

Permitted:
- Standard supportive care (antiemetics, analgesics, bisphosphonates)
- Palliative radiation to non-target lesions (with sponsor notification)

Prohibited:
- Other systemic anticancer therapy
- Live vaccines during treatment
- Strong CYP3A4 inhibitors/inducers (washout ≥ 5 half-lives before Day 1)
- Corticosteroids > 10 mg/day prednisone equivalent (except for irAE management)

7.1 Adverse Event Monitoring

AEs will be graded per NCI CTCAE v5.0 and collected from informed consent
through 30 days (90 days for SAEs) after last dose.

Pregnancy Test (WOCBP)         X           C1 D1       Q3C          X

7.3 Hepatotoxicity Monitoring Protocol

Given the hepatotoxicity signal observed in Phase 1/2:
- LFTs (AST, ALT, ALP, total bilirubin) weekly × 8 weeks, then Q3W
- If ALT/AST > 3× ULN: increase to twice weekly monitoring
- Hy's Law criteria triggers immediate treatment discontinuation
- Liver biopsy recommended for suspected DILI Grade ≥ 3

7.4 Cardiac Safety

- Serial ECGs at screening, C1D1, C1D8, C2D1 (pre-dose and 2h post-dose)
- Centralized ECG reading
- LVEF assessment by echocardiography at screening and Q12W

7.5 Special Populations

- Hepatic impairment: Excluded if baseline ALT/AST > 2.5× ULN (> 5× if liver mets)
- Renal impairment: CrCl ≥ 50 mL/min required; PK sub-study for CrCl 30-50 mL/min
- Elderly (≥ 75): No dose adjustment; safety monitoring intensified

8. EFFICACY ASSESSMENTS

8.1 Tumor Assessments

- CT (chest/abdomen/pelvis) or MRI per RECIST v1.1
- Schedule: Every 6 weeks (±7 days) for first 48 weeks, then every 12 weeks
- Brain MRI: Baseline and as clinically indicated
- Assessment by BICR (primary) and investigator (secondary)

8.2 Response Criteria

Per RECIST v1.1:
- Complete Response (CR): Disappearance of all target/non-target lesions
- Partial Response (PR): ≥ 30% decrease in sum of diameters
- Stable Disease (SD): Neither PR nor PD
- Progressive Disease (PD): ≥ 20% increase in sum of diameters + 5 mm absolute increase

Confirmation: CR and PR require confirmation ≥ 4 weeks after initial assessment

8.3 Patient-Reported Outcomes

- EORTC QLQ-C30 (global quality of life): Day 1 of each cycle, EOT, FU
- EORTC QLQ-LC13 (lung cancer specific): Day 1 of each cycle, EOT, FU
- EQ-5D-5L (health utility): Day 1 Q3C, EOT, FU

9. STATISTICAL CONSIDERATIONS

9.1 Sample Size

Primary endpoint: PFS (BICR per RECIST v1.1)
Assumptions:
- Median PFS control arm: 8.0 months
- Expected hazard ratio: 0.70
- Median PFS experimental arm: ~11.4 months
- Type I error (two-sided): 0.05
- Power: 90%
- Dropout rate: 5%
- Required PFS events: ~420

Sample size: 680 patients (340 per arm)

9.2 Analysis Populations

- ITT: All randomized patients (primary efficacy analysis)
- Per-Protocol: Patients who received ≥ 80% planned dose and had ≥ 1 post-baseline assessment
- Safety: All patients who received ≥ 1 dose of study treatment

9.3 Primary Analysis

PFS will be compared between arms using a stratified log-rank test
(stratification factors: PD-L1 TPS, ECOG PS, geographic region).

Hazard ratio and 95% CI estimated from a stratified Cox proportional
hazards model. Kaplan-Meier methodology for median PFS estimation.

9.4 Interim Analysis

One interim analysis for efficacy (PFS) planned after approximately 60%
of required events (~252 events) using the Lan-DeMets spending function
with O'Brien-Fleming boundary (alpha = 0.0031 at interim, 0.0491 at final).

An independent Data Monitoring Committee (DMC) will review unblinded
interim data.

9.5 Multiplicity Adjustment

Hierarchical testing procedure:
1. PFS (overall population)
2. OS (overall population) — tested only if PFS is significant
3. ORR (overall population) — tested only if OS is significant

Alpha recycling per Maurer-Bretz graphical approach.

9.6 Subgroup Analyses

Pre-specified subgroups:
- PD-L1 TPS (≥ 50% vs. 1-49%)
- ECOG PS (0 vs. 1)
- Age (< 65 vs. ≥ 65)
- Sex
- Geographic region
- TMB (high vs. low, exploratory cutoff)
- Baseline brain metastases (yes vs. no)
