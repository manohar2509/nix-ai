1. SYNOPSIS

Study Title:        RESTORE-HF
Phase:              Phase 2
Study Design:       Randomized, double-blind, dose-ranging, placebo-controlled
Indication:         Heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (HFrEF), NYHA II-III
Study Population:   Adults ≥ 18 years with LVEF ≤ 40%, on stable background therapy
Target Enrollment:  360 patients (90 per arm across 4 arms)
Number of Sites:    ~60 sites in North America and Europe

Treatment Arms:
  Arm A: Cardionix 50 mg PO QD + Standard of Care (SoC)
  Arm B: Cardionix 100 mg PO QD + SoC
  Arm C: Cardionix 200 mg PO QD + SoC
  Arm D: Placebo PO QD + SoC

Primary Endpoint:   Change from baseline in NT-proBNP at Week 24
Key Secondary:      Change in LVEF (echocardiography), 6-Minute Walk Distance (6MWD),
                    KCCQ-12 composite score, composite of CV death or HF hospitalization

Study Duration:     ~36 months (18 months enrollment + 6 months treatment + 12 months FU)
Treatment Duration: 24 weeks

2. INTRODUCTION AND BACKGROUND

2.1 Disease Background

Heart failure (HF) affects over 64 million people globally. Despite guideline-directed
medical therapy (GDMT) including ACE inhibitors/ARBs, beta-blockers, mineralocorticoid
receptor antagonists, and SGLT2 inhibitors, residual morbidity and mortality remain
substantial. The 5-year mortality rate for HFrEF is approximately 50%.

2.2 Investigational Product: Cardionix (NIX-7290)

Cardionix is a first-in-class, orally bioavailable activator of mitochondrial
complex I in cardiomyocytes. It targets the NDUFS4 subunit to enhance oxidative
phosphorylation efficiency, reducing reactive oxygen species (ROS) generation
while improving ATP production in failing myocardium.

Mechanism of Action:
- Allosteric activation of mitochondrial Complex I (NADH:ubiquinone oxidoreductase)
- Enhanced ATP synthesis efficiency (+25-35% in preclinical models)
- Reduced mitochondrial ROS generation (-40% in vitro)
- Improved calcium handling in cardiomyocytes
- Anti-fibrotic effects via TGF-β pathway modulation

2.3 Preclinical Data

In canine tachypacing-induced HF model:
- LVEF improvement: +12% (Cardionix) vs. +2% (vehicle), p < 0.001
- BNP reduction: -55% (Cardionix) vs. -8% (vehicle), p < 0.001
- Exercise capacity: +35% increase in treadmill time
- Myocardial fibrosis: -40% (collagen volume fraction)

In rat MI model (post-LAD ligation):
- Cardionix (10 mg/kg) prevented adverse remodeling at 8 weeks
- LV end-diastolic diameter: 5.8 mm vs. 7.2 mm (vehicle), p < 0.01
- Survival at 12 weeks: 89% vs. 62%, p = 0.03

2.4 Phase 1 Data (Study NIX-CV-2024-001)

Single Ascending Dose (SAD, n=40, healthy volunteers):
- Well tolerated up to 400 mg single dose
- Tmax: 2-3 hours; T1/2: 14-18 hours (supports QD dosing)
- No QTc prolongation at supratherapeutic doses

Multiple Ascending Dose (MAD, n=36, HFrEF patients):
- 50 mg, 100 mg, 200 mg QD × 14 days
- Dose-proportional PK
- NT-proBNP reduction: -18% (50 mg), -32% (100 mg), -41% (200 mg) at Day 14
- No dose-limiting toxicities
- Most common AEs: mild headache (15%), dizziness (8%), nausea (6%)

3. STUDY OBJECTIVES AND ENDPOINTS

3.1 Primary Objective
To evaluate the dose-response of Cardionix on NT-proBNP reduction in HFrEF patients.

Primary Endpoint: Percent change from baseline in NT-proBNP at Week 24.

3.2 Key Secondary Objectives and Endpoints

  Objective                                    Endpoint
  ─────────────────────────────────────────    ──────────────────────────────
  Evaluate cardiac function                    Change in LVEF at Week 24 (echo)
  Evaluate functional capacity                 Change in 6MWD at Week 24
  Assess patient symptoms/QoL                  KCCQ-12 score change at Week 24
  Evaluate clinical events                     Composite: CV death or HF hosp.
  Characterize PK                              Population PK model (Cmin, AUC)
  Evaluate safety & tolerability               AEs, SAEs, vital signs, ECG

3.3 Exploratory Objectives

- Cardiac biomarkers: hs-cTnT, sST2, galectin-3 kinetics
- Echocardiographic remodeling: LVEDVi, LVESVi, GLS
- Cardiac MRI substudy (n=60): myocardial energetics via 31P-MRS
- Wearable device-derived metrics: daily step count, HR variability
- Dose-exposure-response modeling for Phase 3 dose selection

4. STUDY DESIGN

4.1 Overall Design

Phase 2, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, dose-ranging study.
360 patients randomized 1:1:1:1 to Cardionix 50 mg, 100 mg, 200 mg, or placebo.

Randomization stratified by:
- Baseline NT-proBNP (< 1000 vs. ≥ 1000 pg/mL)
- NYHA class (II vs. III)
- Background SGLT2i use (yes vs. no)

4.2 Study Periods

Screening:         Up to 4 weeks (includes GDMT optimization period)
Treatment:         24 weeks (with visits at Weeks 2, 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24)
Safety Follow-up:  4 weeks after last dose (Week 28)
Extended Follow-up: 12 months after last dose for clinical events

4.3 Adaptive Design Element

A pre-planned interim analysis after 50% of patients complete Week 12
will be conducted by an independent Data Monitoring Committee (DMC).
The DMC may:
1. Recommend dropping a dose arm for futility (conditional power < 20%)
2. Recommend increasing sample size (up to 480 total) for promising doses
3. Recommend study termination for safety concerns

This adaptive element follows FDA Guidance on Adaptive Designs (2019).

5. STUDY POPULATION

5.1 Inclusion Criteria

  1. Age ≥ 18 years
  2. Chronic HF (NYHA Class II or III) for ≥ 3 months
  3. LVEF ≤ 40% documented by echocardiography within 6 months
  4. NT-proBNP ≥ 400 pg/mL (≥ 800 pg/mL if atrial fibrillation)
  5. Stable, optimized GDMT for ≥ 4 weeks prior to randomization:
     a. ACEi/ARB/ARNI at ≥ 50% target dose (unless contraindicated/intolerant)
     b. Evidence-based beta-blocker at ≥ 50% target dose
     c. MRA (unless contraindicated)
     d. SGLT2i recommended but not required
  6. eGFR ≥ 30 mL/min/1.73 m² (CKD-EPI)
  7. Systolic BP ≥ 100 mmHg
  8. Written informed consent

5.2 Exclusion Criteria

  1. Acute decompensated HF requiring IV therapy within 4 weeks
  2. Cardiac surgery or PCI within 3 months
  3. Planned cardiac device implantation (CRT, LVAD) during study
  4. Existing CRT with optimization < 3 months prior
  5. Primary hypertrophic, restrictive, or infiltrative cardiomyopathy
  6. Severe uncorrected valvular disease
  7. Sustained ventricular tachycardia or ventricular fibrillation within 3 months
  8. Severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh C)
  9. Known mitochondrial disease
  10. Active malignancy (non-melanoma skin cancer excepted)
  11. Life expectancy < 12 months for non-cardiac reasons
  12. Pregnancy, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant
  13. Participation in another interventional trial within 30 days

6. TREATMENT PLAN

6.1 Investigational Medicinal Product

Drug:               Cardionix (NIX-7290)
Formulations:       Capsule, 50 mg and 100 mg
Doses:              50 mg QD, 100 mg QD, 200 mg QD
Route:              Oral
Schedule:           Once daily, with breakfast
Duration:           24 weeks

6.2 Background Standard of Care

All patients must remain on stable GDMT throughout the treatment period.
Dose adjustments to background HF therapy are permitted only for safety reasons
and must be documented in the eCRF.

Required background therapy (unless documented contraindication/intolerance):
- ACEi/ARB/ARNI
- Beta-blocker (carvedilol, bisoprolol, or metoprolol succinate)
- MRA (spironolactone or eplerenone)
- SGLT2i (dapagliflozin or empagliflozin) — strongly recommended

6.3 Prohibited Medications

- Other investigational drugs
- IV inotropes (except for acute decompensation management)
- Other mitochondrial-targeted therapies (CoQ10 supplements > 200 mg/day)

7. SAFETY ASSESSMENTS

7.1 Adverse Event Monitoring

All AEs graded per CTCAE v5.0. Collection from first dose through 28 days
after last dose. SAEs collected through 12 months follow-up.

7.2 Cardiac Safety

- Continuous Holter monitoring at baseline, Week 4, and Week 24 (48 hours)
- 12-lead ECG at each visit (pre-dose)
- Troponin (hs-cTnT) at each study visit
- Echocardiography: baseline, Week 12, Week 24 (central reading)

7.3 Hepatic Safety

- Liver function tests (ALT, AST, ALP, bilirubin) at each visit
- Hy's Law monitoring per FDA DILI guidance
- If ALT > 3× ULN: recheck within 72 hours and weekly until resolved

7.4 Renal Safety

- Serum creatinine and eGFR at each visit
- If eGFR declines > 25% from baseline: increase monitoring frequency
- If eGFR < 20 mL/min: consider dose modification or discontinuation

7.5 Clinical Events Committee (CEC)

An independent, blinded CEC will adjudicate:
- All deaths (CV vs. non-CV)
- All hospitalizations (HF vs. non-HF)
- All stroke events
- All MI events

8. EFFICACY ASSESSMENTS

8.1 NT-proBNP (Primary)

- Central laboratory measurement at: screening, baseline, Weeks 2, 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24
- Fasting morning sample (before daily dose)
- Roche Elecsys proBNP II assay

8.2 Echocardiography

- 2D transthoracic echocardiography per ASE/EACVI guidelines
- Measurements: LVEF (biplane Simpson's), LVEDVi, LVESVi, GLS
- Central core lab reading (blinded)
- Schedule: screening, Week 12, Week 24

8.3 Functional Capacity

- 6-Minute Walk Test per ATS guidelines
- Schedule: baseline, Week 12, Week 24
- Performed before other study procedures on visit day

8.4 Patient-Reported Outcomes

- KCCQ-12 (Kansas City Cardiomyopathy Questionnaire): baseline, Weeks 4, 12, 24
- EQ-5D-5L: baseline, Weeks 12, 24
- PGI-S and PGI-C (Patient Global Impression): Weeks 12, 24

9. STATISTICAL CONSIDERATIONS

9.1 Sample Size

Primary endpoint: Percent change from baseline in NT-proBNP at Week 24
Assumptions:
- Placebo change: -5% (natural variability on GDMT)
- Clinically meaningful difference: ≥ 20% additional reduction
- SD of percent change: ~45%
- Type I error: 0.05 (two-sided, Dunnett adjustment for 3 comparisons)
- Power: 80% for each pairwise comparison

Required: 80 evaluable patients per arm → 90 randomized per arm (10% dropout)
Total: 360 patients

9.2 Analysis Populations

- mITT: All randomized patients who received ≥ 1 dose and have ≥ 1 post-baseline NT-proBNP
- Safety: All patients who received ≥ 1 dose
- PK: All patients with ≥ 1 evaluable PK sample

9.3 Primary Analysis

MMRM (Mixed Model for Repeated Measures) with:
- Fixed effects: treatment group, visit, treatment × visit, baseline NT-proBNP (log), stratification factors
- Unstructured covariance matrix
- Primary contrast: each Cardionix dose vs. placebo at Week 24
- Dunnett's procedure for multiplicity control

9.4 Key Secondary Analyses

- LVEF, 6MWD, KCCQ-12: MMRM similar to primary
- Composite endpoint (CV death or HF hospitalization): Cox proportional hazards,
  stratified by randomization factors
- All tested in hierarchical order following primary endpoint significance

9.5 Dose-Response Modeling

- MCP-Mod methodology per FDA/EMA qualification guidance
- Candidate dose-response shapes: linear, Emax, exponential, logistic
- Used to inform dose selection for Phase 3
