1. SYNOPSIS

Study Title:        CLARITY-AD Phase 1
Phase:              Phase 1 (dose escalation + expansion)
Study Design:       Open-label, 3+3 dose escalation with expansion cohorts
Indication:         Early-stage Alzheimer's disease (prodromal AD or mild AD dementia)
Study Population:   Adults 50-85 years with biomarker-confirmed amyloid pathology
Target Enrollment:  Part A: ~30 patients (escalation)
                    Part B: ~60 patients (expansion at RP2D)
Number of Sites:    ~20 specialized memory centers in the United States

Treatment:
  Part A (Escalation): Neuronix 5 mg, 15 mg, 30 mg, 60 mg, 120 mg SC Q2W
  Part B (Expansion):  Neuronix at RP2D SC Q2W

Primary Endpoint:   Dose-limiting toxicities (DLTs) and maximum tolerated dose (MTD)
Key Secondary:      PK parameters, CSF biomarkers (Aβ42, p-tau181, NfL),
                    amyloid PET (Centiloid), cognitive assessments (ADAS-Cog 13, CDR-SB)

Study Duration:     ~48 months total
Treatment Duration: 52 weeks

2. INTRODUCTION AND BACKGROUND

2.1 Disease Background

Alzheimer's disease (AD) affects approximately 55 million people worldwide, with
prevalence projected to triple by 2050. AD is characterized by progressive
neurodegeneration driven by:
- Extracellular amyloid-β (Aβ) plaque accumulation
- Intracellular neurofibrillary tangles (hyperphosphorylated tau)
- Neuroinflammation (microglial activation)
- Synaptic dysfunction and neuronal loss

Recent anti-amyloid antibodies (lecanemab, donanemab) have demonstrated modest
clinical benefit but carry significant risks of amyloid-related imaging
abnormalities (ARIA), limiting their broad applicability.

2.2 Investigational Product: Neuronix (NIX-9150)

Neuronix is a novel, subcutaneously administered, bispecific antibody-peptide
conjugate that simultaneously targets:

1. Soluble Aβ oligomers (primary neurotoxic species)
2. TREM2 receptor activation (anti-inflammatory microglial phenotype)

This dual mechanism aims to both clear the toxic amyloid species driving
neurodegeneration AND shift microglial response from pro-inflammatory (M1)
to neuroprotective (M2) phenotype — addressing two key pathological drivers
simultaneously.

Mechanism of Action:
- Fab arm 1: High-affinity binding to Aβ oligomers (KD = 45 pM)
- Fab arm 2: Agonistic binding to TREM2 (activating signal)
- Conjugated neuroprotective peptide: blood-brain barrier shuttle (transferrin
  receptor-mediated transcytosis)
- Enhanced microglial phagocytosis of Aβ plaques
- Reduced NLRP3 inflammasome activation
- Preserved synaptic density (preclinical data)

2.3 Preclinical Data

5xFAD transgenic mouse model:
- Amyloid plaque burden: -65% (Neuronix) vs. -30% (anti-Aβ only), p < 0.001
- Microglial activation (Iba1): -45% in cortex and hippocampus
- Synaptic markers (PSD-95, synaptophysin): preserved at wild-type levels
- Morris Water Maze: latency normalized to wild-type performance
- ARIA-like MRI findings: 0% (Neuronix) vs. 15% (anti-Aβ only)

NHP (cynomolgus macaque) toxicology:
- 26-week GLP study, SC dosing up to 100 mg/kg Q2W
- NOAEL: 30 mg/kg (corresponding to human ~120 mg)
- No CNS toxicity, no ARIA signals on brain MRI
- CSF penetration: ~2% of plasma levels (therapeutic range based on PD modeling)
- Anti-drug antibodies: 8% incidence, non-neutralizing, no impact on PK

2.4 Rationale

Current anti-amyloid therapies achieve plaque clearance but do not adequately
address the neuroinflammatory component of AD pathology. TREM2 loss-of-function
variants (R47H) are among the strongest genetic risk factors for late-onset AD,
and TREM2 agonism has emerged as a promising therapeutic strategy.

By combining amyloid clearance with TREM2-mediated neuroprotection in a single
molecule, Neuronix is designed to provide superior efficacy with a potentially
improved safety profile (reduced ARIA risk through controlled microglial activation).

3. STUDY OBJECTIVES AND ENDPOINTS

3.1 Primary Objectives

Part A: To determine the MTD and/or RP2D of Neuronix in early-stage AD patients.
Part B: To further evaluate safety and tolerability at the RP2D.

Primary Endpoints:
- Incidence and severity of DLTs (DLT evaluation period: 28 days after first dose)
- Incidence, severity, and relatedness of treatment-emergent AEs (TEAEs)

3.2 Secondary Objectives and Endpoints

  Objective                                    Endpoint
  ─────────────────────────────────────────    ──────────────────────────────
  Characterize PK                              Cmax, AUC, T½, clearance, Vd
  Evaluate target engagement                   CSF Aβ42, Aβ42/40 ratio
  Assess tau pathology                         CSF p-tau181, p-tau217, t-tau
  Evaluate neurodegeneration                   CSF NfL, plasma NfL
  Assess amyloid clearance                     Amyloid PET (Centiloid change)
  Preliminary cognitive efficacy               ADAS-Cog 13, CDR-SB, MMSE
  Evaluate immunogenicity                      ADA incidence and titers

3.3 Exploratory Objectives

- Tau PET (18F-flortaucipir) change at 52 weeks (optional)
- Volumetric MRI (hippocampal/whole brain atrophy rates)
- Digital biomarkers (speech patterns, typing speed, actigraphy)
- Plasma p-tau217 as non-invasive monitoring tool
- Single-cell RNA-seq of CSF immune cells (optional lumbar puncture substudy)
- Gut microbiome composition and relationship to drug response

4. STUDY DESIGN

4.1 Part A: Dose Escalation (3+3 Design)

Dose Level 1:  5 mg SC Q2W
Dose Level 2:  15 mg SC Q2W
Dose Level 3:  30 mg SC Q2W
Dose Level 4:  60 mg SC Q2W
Dose Level 5:  120 mg SC Q2W

DLT evaluation period: 28 days after first dose.
Dose escalation rules:
- 0/3 DLTs → escalate to next dose level
- 1/3 DLTs → expand to 6 patients
- ≤ 1/6 DLTs → escalate
- ≥ 2/6 DLTs → dose level exceeds MTD; previous level is MTD

DLT Definitions:
- Grade ≥ 3 AE considered related to Neuronix (excluding injection site reactions)
- ARIA-E (moderate or severe, symptomatic)
- ARIA-H (> 2 microhemorrhages or any macrohemorrhage)
- Grade ≥ 3 infusion/injection-related reaction
- Any AE leading to treatment discontinuation during DLT period

4.2 Part B: Dose Expansion

- RP2D (determined from Part A) × 52 weeks
- Two expansion cohorts:
  Cohort B1 (n=30): Prodromal AD (CDR 0.5)
  Cohort B2 (n=30): Mild AD dementia (CDR 1.0)

4.3 MRI Safety Monitoring Protocol

Given ARIA risk with anti-amyloid therapies:
- Brain MRI (FLAIR + T2*-GRE) at: screening, Week 4, 8, 16, 24, 36, 52
- Standardized ARIA reading by central neuroradiology core lab
- ARIA management algorithm (see Section 7.3)
- APOE ε4 genotyping mandatory (stratification factor for ARIA risk)

5. STUDY POPULATION

5.1 Inclusion Criteria

  1. Age 50-85 years (inclusive)
  2. Clinical diagnosis of:
     a. Prodromal AD (CDR Global Score = 0.5, Memory Box ≥ 0.5), OR
     b. Mild AD dementia (CDR Global Score = 1.0)
  3. MMSE score 20-28 (inclusive)
  4. Amyloid-positive by EITHER:
     a. Amyloid PET (visual read positive), OR
     b. CSF Aβ42 < 600 pg/mL (or Aβ42/40 ratio < 0.089)
  5. APOE genotype documented (not exclusionary; used for ARIA risk stratification)
  6. Stable doses of AD medications (cholinesterase inhibitors, memantine)
     for ≥ 3 months prior to screening. Initiation or dose changes during
     the study are not permitted.
  7. Reliable study partner who spends ≥ 10 hours/week with patient
  8. Adequate visual and auditory acuity for neuropsychological testing
  9. Written informed consent (patient and study partner)

5.2 Exclusion Criteria

  1. Any significant neurological condition other than AD
  2. History of seizures within 5 years
  3. Brain MRI at screening showing:
     a. > 4 cerebral microhemorrhages
     b. Any area of superficial siderosis
     c. Evidence of prior macrohemorrhage
     d. > 1 lacunar infarct
     e. Any stroke within 12 months
  4. Current treatment with anti-amyloid therapy (lecanemab, donanemab, aducanumab)
     or treatment within 6 months of screening
  5. Treatment with immunosuppressants within 12 months
  6. Anticoagulant therapy (antiplatelet agents allowed except during LP windows)
  7. Uncontrolled hypertension (SBP > 160 or DBP > 100 mmHg)
  8. History of autoimmune disease
  9. Known immunodeficiency
  10. Severe renal impairment (eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73 m²)
  11. Hepatic impairment (ALT/AST > 2× ULN)
  12. Suicidal ideation (C-SSRS score ≥ 4) within 12 months
  13. MRI contraindications (pacemaker, certain metallic implants)

6. TREATMENT PLAN

6.1 Investigational Medicinal Product

Drug:               Neuronix (NIX-9150)
Formulation:        Solution for subcutaneous injection, 50 mg/mL
Dose levels:        5 mg, 15 mg, 30 mg, 60 mg, 120 mg
Route:              Subcutaneous (abdomen, thigh, or upper arm)
Schedule:           Every 2 weeks (Q2W), administered at study site
Duration:           Part A: 12 weeks (6 doses) + extension to 52 weeks if tolerated
                    Part B: 52 weeks (26 doses)

6.2 Administration

- All doses administered by qualified site staff
- Post-injection observation: 60 minutes (Part A), 30 minutes (Part B)
- Injection site rotation required

6.3 Concomitant Medications

Permitted (stable dose required):
- Cholinesterase inhibitors (donepezil, rivastigmine, galantamine)
- Memantine
- SSRIs/SNRIs for depression/anxiety (stable ≥ 3 months)
- Low-dose aspirin (≤ 325 mg/day)

Prohibited:
- Anti-amyloid antibodies
- Other investigational drugs
- Immunosuppressants (including systemic corticosteroids > 10 mg/day prednisone)
- Anticoagulants (warfarin, DOACs) — 7-day washout needed for lumbar puncture

7. SAFETY ASSESSMENTS

7.1 General Safety

AE collection from informed consent through 12 weeks after last dose.
SAE collection through 24 weeks after last dose.
AE grading per NCI CTCAE v5.0.

7.2 Key Safety Evaluations

Assessment                     Schedule
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Physical/Neurological Exam     Each visit
Vital Signs                    Each visit (pre- and post-dose)
12-Lead ECG                    Screening, Weeks 4, 12, 24, 52
C-SSRS (Suicidality)          Screening, Weeks 4, 12, 24, 36, 52
Hematology/Chemistry           Each visit
Urinalysis                     Screening, Q12W
Brain MRI (ARIA monitoring)    Screen, Wks 4, 8, 16, 24, 36, 52
Immunogenicity (ADA)           Pre-dose Wks 1, 4, 12, 24, 52 + FU

7.3 ARIA Management Algorithm

ARIA-E (Edema):
  Mild (radiographic only):         Continue dosing, recheck MRI in 4 weeks
  Moderate (mild symptoms):         Hold dosing, recheck MRI Q4W until resolved,
                                    may resume at same dose
  Severe (significant symptoms):    Permanent discontinuation

ARIA-H (Hemorrhage):
  1-2 new microhemorrhages:        Continue dosing, recheck MRI in 4 weeks
  3-4 new microhemorrhages:        Hold dosing, recheck MRI Q4W
  > 4 new or any macrohemorrhage:  Permanent discontinuation
  Symptomatic hemorrhage:           Permanent discontinuation, urgent neuroimaging

7.4 Immunogenicity Monitoring

- Anti-drug antibody (ADA) screening and confirmatory assay
- Neutralizing antibody (NAb) characterization if ADA positive
- PK correlation analysis for ADA-positive patients

9. STATISTICAL CONSIDERATIONS

9.1 Sample Size

Part A (Dose Escalation):
- 3+3 design: 3-6 patients per dose level, up to 5 levels = 15-30 patients
- Standard justification per FDA Phase 1 guidance

Part B (Dose Expansion):
- 30 patients per CDR subgroup (0.5 and 1.0) = 60 total
- Provides ≥ 80% probability of detecting ≥ 1 AE occurring at ≥ 5% incidence
- Exploratory efficacy: 80% power to detect 25% reduction in CSF p-tau181
  (SD = 40%, two-sided α = 0.10)

9.2 Analysis Populations

- DLT-evaluable: Patients completing DLT evaluation period (Part A)
- Safety: All patients receiving ≥ 1 dose (Parts A+B)
- PK: All patients with ≥ 1 evaluable PK sample
- Biomarker: All patients with baseline and ≥ 1 post-baseline biomarker

9.3 Safety Analysis

- Descriptive statistics for TEAEs, SAEs, DLTs by dose level
- Exposure-safety analyses (dose/concentration vs. ARIA incidence)
- Kaplan-Meier for time to first ARIA event

9.4 PK Analysis

- Non-compartmental analysis (NCA) for single-dose PK
- Population PK modeling (nonlinear mixed effects)
- Dose proportionality assessment (power model)

9.5 Preliminary Efficacy Analysis (Part B)

- Within-group paired t-tests for biomarker changes from baseline
- MMRM for cognitive endpoints
- All efficacy analyses considered exploratory (not powered for significance)

10. REGULATORY AND ETHICAL CONSIDERATIONS

10.1 Regulatory Compliance

This study will be conducted per:
- ICH E6(R2) Good Clinical Practice
- ICH E8(R1) General Considerations for Clinical Studies
- ICH M3(R2) Nonclinical Safety Studies
- FDA Guidance: Early Alzheimer's Disease — Developing Drugs for Treatment (2018)
- FDA Guidance: Suicidal Ideation and Behavior (2012)
- 21 CFR Parts 11, 50, 56, 312, 361

10.2 Informed Consent

Due to the cognitive impairment inherent in the study population:
- Both patient AND study partner must provide written informed consent
- Capacity to consent assessed by qualified investigator
- If capacity is questionable, legally authorized representative (LAR)
  must co-sign consent per local regulations
- Re-consent at 6 months and if significant cognitive decline noted

10.3 Ethics Committee

- Central IRB (Advarra) for US sites
- Protocol, ICF, recruitment materials must be approved before enrollment
- Annual continuing review

10.4 Data Protection

- HIPAA compliance (US)
- Neuroimaging data de-identified and stored in XNAT repository
- Genetic data (APOE) stored separately with additional consent
- Data retention: 15 years per FDA requirements for biologics
