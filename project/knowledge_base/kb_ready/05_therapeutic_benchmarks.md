# Therapeutic Area Benchmarks — Protocol Quality Standards

Evidence-based benchmarks by therapeutic area for protocol
quality assessment, endpoints, sample sizes, and success rates.


## Oncology
**Solid Tumors:**
  **Phase 3 Benchmarks:**
    **Typical Sample Size:**
      - Range: 300-1200
      - Note: Varies by effect size and event rate
    **Typical Duration Months:**
      - Treatment: 12-36
      - Follow Up: 24-60+
    **Common Primary Endpoints:**
      - Overall Survival (OS)
      - Progression-Free Survival (PFS)
      - Objective Response Rate (ORR) — for accelerated approval
    **Standard Secondary Endpoints:**
      - Duration of Response (DoR)
      - Disease Control Rate (DCR)
      - Time to Response
      - Patient-Reported Outcomes (PROs)
      - Health-Related Quality of Life (HRQoL)
    - Response Criteria: RECIST v1.1 (Response Evaluation Criteria in Solid Tumors)
    **Comparator Standards:**
      - First Line: Standard of care (often chemo ± immunotherapy)
      - Second Line Plus: Investigator's choice or best available therapy
      - Note: Placebo alone rarely acceptable unless add-on design
    **Success Rates:**
      - Phase 1 To Approval: 5-10%
      - Phase 3 Success: 35-50%
      - Median Approval Timeline Months: 48
    **Common Deficiencies:**
      - Insufficient OS follow-up
      - Lack of patient-reported outcomes
      - Inadequate biomarker strategy
      - Non-representative patient population
      - Missing dose optimisation data
  **Immuno Oncology Specific:**
    **Additional Endpoints:**
      - Immune-related Response Criteria (iRECIST)
      - TMB (Tumor Mutational Burden)
      - PD-L1 expression cutoffs
    **Special Considerations:**
      - Pseudoprogression assessment per iRECIST
      - Delayed separation of KM curves expected
      - Restricted mean survival time (RMST) may be more appropriate than HR when non-proportional hazards
      - Correlative biomarker studies essential
      - Immune-related adverse events monitoring plan
**Haematology:**
  **Phase 3 Benchmarks:**
    **Typical Sample Size:**
      - Range: 200-800
    **Common Primary Endpoints:**
      - Overall Survival
      - Event-Free Survival (EFS)
      - Complete Remission Rate (CR/CRi)
      - Minimal Residual Disease (MRD) negativity
    **Disease Specific Criteria:**
      - Lymphoma: Lugano Classification (Cheson 2014)
      - Myeloma: IMWG Uniform Response Criteria
      - Leukemia: ELN 2022 Recommendations (AML), iwCLL criteria (CLL)
      - Mds: IWG 2006 criteria


## Cardiovascular
**Cv Outcome Trials:**
  **Phase 3 Benchmarks:**
    **Typical Sample Size:**
      - Range: 5000-15000
      - Note: Event-driven designs
    **Typical Duration Months:**
      - Treatment: 24-48
      - Follow Up: 36-60
    - Primary Endpoint: 3-point MACE (CV death + non-fatal MI + non-fatal stroke)
    **Alternative Composites:**
      - 4-point MACE (adds hospitalisation for unstable angina)
      - Expanded MACE (adds HF hospitalisation, revascularisation)
    **Secondary Endpoints:**
      - Individual MACE components
      - All-cause mortality
      - Heart failure hospitalisation
      - Renal composite endpoint
    **Design Requirements:**
      - Event-driven with pre-specified event count
      - Independent Clinical Events Committee (CEC) for adjudication
      - Central lab for biomarkers (troponin, NT-proBNP)
      - Enrichment for high-risk population
    **Success Rates:**
      - Phase 3 Success: 55-65%
      - Note: Higher than oncology due to established endpoints
**Heart Failure:**
  **Primary Endpoints:**
    - CV death + HF hospitalisation composite
    - NT-proBNP change from baseline
    - KCCQ (Kansas City Cardiomyopathy Questionnaire)
  - Classification: NYHA Class I-IV
  **Subgroups:**
    - HFrEF (EF ≤40%)
    - HFmrEF (EF 41-49%)
    - HFpEF (EF ≥50%)


## Neurology
**Alzheimers Disease:**
  **Phase 3 Benchmarks:**
    **Typical Sample Size:**
      - Range: 1500-3000
    **Typical Duration Months:**
      - Treatment: 18-24
      - Follow Up: 24-36
    **Co Primary Endpoints:**
      - Cognitive: ADAS-Cog 14 or CDR-SB
      - Functional: ADCS-ADL or CDR-SB global
    **Biomarker Requirements:**
      - Amyloid PET positivity OR CSF Aβ42/p-tau ratio
      - Tau PET for staging (emerging)
    **Design Considerations:**
      - Disease modification vs symptomatic claims — different endpoint and duration requirements
      - Mandatory biomarker confirmation of AD pathology
      - Long duration (≥18 months) for disease modification
      - High screen failure rate (30-50%) due to biomarker requirements
    **Success Rates:**
      - Phase 3 Success: 15-20%
      - Note: Historically very low; improving with biomarker-selected populations
**Multiple Sclerosis:**
  **Primary Endpoints:**
    - Annualised relapse rate (ARR)
    - 12-week confirmed disability progression (CDP)
    - MRI: T2 lesion volume, gadolinium-enhancing lesions
  - Typical Duration Months: 24
  - Comparator: Active comparator required (interferon-beta or teriflunomide)


## Immunology Inflammation
**Rheumatoid Arthritis:**
  **Primary Endpoints:**
    - ACR20/50/70 response rates
    - DAS28-CRP remission (< 2.6)
    - HAQ-DI (function)
  **Typical Sample Size:**
    - Range: 400-800
  - Design: Randomised, double-blind, with rescue therapy allowed
**Atopic Dermatitis:**
  **Primary Endpoints:**
    - IGA 0/1 with ≥2-grade improvement
    - EASI-75 (75% improvement in EASI score)
  - Co Primary: Often IGA + EASI-75
  **Secondary:**
    - Pruritus NRS
    - DLQI
    - SCORAD
    - BSA affected


## Rare Diseases
**General Benchmarks:**
  **Typical Sample Size:**
    - Range: 20-150
    - Note: Often single-arm or crossover
  **Design Flexibility:**
    - Single-arm with historical/external comparator acceptable
    - Crossover designs to maximise information per patient
    - Natural history studies as control
    - N-of-1 trials in ultra-rare populations
    - Bayesian adaptive designs recommended
  **Endpoint Considerations:**
    - Patient-relevant functional endpoints prioritised
    - Biomarker endpoints acceptable for accelerated/conditional approval
    - Composite endpoints to increase statistical power
    - Clinician Global Impression of Change (CGI-C) accepted
  **Regulatory Incentives:**
    - Fda: Orphan Drug Act: 7 years exclusivity, tax credits, fee waivers
    - Ema: Orphan designation: 10 years market exclusivity, protocol assistance, fee reductions
    - Pmda: Orphan designation: 10 years exclusivity, priority review, tax credits


## Metabolic Endocrine
**Diabetes Type 2:**
  **Primary Endpoints:**
    - HbA1c change from baseline at 24-26 weeks
  **Key Secondary:**
    - Fasting plasma glucose
    - Body weight
    - Proportion achieving HbA1c <7%
  **Cv Safety Requirement:**
    - Description: FDA requires CVOT meta-analysis or dedicated CVOT
    - Threshold: Upper bound of 95% CI for MACE HR must be <1.8 pre-approval, <1.3 post-approval
    - Reference: FDA Guidance 2008 (updated 2020)
  - Typical Duration Months: 26
  - Design: Add-on to metformin or active comparator
**Obesity:**
  **Primary Endpoints:**
    - % change in body weight from baseline
    - Proportion achieving ≥5% weight loss
  - Co Primary: Often mean % weight change + responder analysis
  - Duration Months: 52


## Respiratory
**Asthma:**
  **Phase 3 Benchmarks:**
    **Typical Sample Size:**
      - Range: 400-1500
      - Note: Higher for exacerbation trials
    **Typical Duration Months:**
      - Treatment: 12-52
      - Note: 12 weeks for bronchodilator, 52 weeks for controller
    **Primary Endpoints:**
      - FEV1 improvement from baseline (bronchodilators)
      - Annualised exacerbation rate reduction (controllers)
    **Secondary Endpoints:**
      - ACQ (Asthma Control Questionnaire)
      - AQLQ (Asthma Quality of Life Questionnaire)
      - Rescue medication use
      - Peak expiratory flow rate
    **Design Standards:**
      - Randomised, double-blind, parallel group
      - Step-down design for steroid-sparing claims
      - Add-on to standard background therapy
      - Minimum 12 weeks for bronchodilator efficacy
      - Minimum 52 weeks for exacerbation prevention
      - Severity stratification: mild, moderate, severe, uncontrolled
    **Comparator Standards:**
      - Biologic Therapies: Add-on to high-dose ICS/LABA
      - Bronchodilators: Add-on to ICS, active comparator preferred
    **Regulatory Expectations:**
      - Fda: FEV1 + exacerbation rate, population-specific biomarker (eosinophils, FeNO, IgE)
      - Ema: EMA/CHMP guideline: FEV1, exacerbation rate, QoL; biomarker-defined subgroups
    **Success Rates:**
      - Phase 3 Success: 55-65%
**Copd:**
  **Phase 3 Benchmarks:**
    **Typical Sample Size:**
      - Range: 500-10000
      - Note: Larger for exacerbation outcomes
    **Typical Duration Months:**
      - Treatment: 24-52
    **Primary Endpoints:**
      - Trough FEV1 (bronchodilators)
      - Exacerbation rate reduction (anti-inflammatory)
      - All-cause mortality (CVOT-style for some agents)
    **Secondary Endpoints:**
      - SGRQ (St George's Respiratory Questionnaire)
      - CAT (COPD Assessment Test)
      - 6-minute walk distance
      - Time to first exacerbation
    **Design Standards:**
      - GOLD classification for severity stratification
      - Active comparator preferred (LABA, LAMA, ICS/LABA)
      - Minimum 12 weeks for bronchodilator efficacy
      - Minimum 52 weeks for exacerbation prevention
    **Success Rates:**
      - Phase 3 Success: 50-60%


## Infectious Disease
**Antibacterial Agents:**
  **Phase 3 Benchmarks:**
    **Typical Sample Size:**
      - Range: 300-1200
      - Note: Per infection type
    **Typical Duration Months:**
      - Treatment: 0.5-2
      - Note: Short treatment courses
    **Primary Endpoints:**
      - Clinical cure at test-of-cure visit
      - Microbiological eradication
      - 28-day all-cause mortality (for serious infections like HAP/VAP)
    **Secondary Endpoints:**
      - Per-pathogen response rate
      - Clinical relapse rate
      - Resistance emergence during treatment
    **Design Standards:**
      - Non-inferiority design with FDA/EMA-agreed margin (typically 10% or 15%)
      - Enroll patients with confirmed pathogen (randomise on clinical diagnosis, analyse microbiological per-pathogen)
      - PK/PD target attainment analysis
      - Site-specific infection trials: ABSSSI, CABP, cUTI, cIAI, HAP/VAP, BSI
      - Drug-resistant pathogen enrichment strategy
    **Regulatory Expectations:**
      - Fda: FDA LPAD pathway for limited population antibacterials; streamlined program for qualified infectious disease product (QIDP)
      - Ema: EMA addendum on antibacterials: PK/PD modeling, resistance monitoring
      - Global: AMR (Antimicrobial Resistance) incentives: transferable exclusivity vouchers (EU), QIDP designation (FDA)
**Hiv:**
  **Phase 3 Benchmarks:**
    **Typical Sample Size:**
      - Range: 300-700
    **Primary Endpoints:**
      - HIV-1 RNA <50 copies/mL at Week 48 (FDA Snapshot analysis)
      - HIV-1 RNA <200 copies/mL (secondary)
    **Secondary Endpoints:**
      - CD4 count change
      - Resistance at virologic failure
      - Safety/tolerability
      - Bone and renal biomarkers
    **Design Standards:**
      - Non-inferiority design vs standard of care ART regimen
      - 48-week primary analysis, 96-week supportive
      - Switch study design for treatment-experienced patients
      - FDA snapshot algorithm for primary analysis
      - Both treatment-naive and treatment-experienced populations
**Hepatitis:**
  **Phase 3 Benchmarks:**
    **Primary Endpoints:**
      - SVR12 (Sustained Virological Response at 12 weeks post-treatment) for HCV
      - HBsAg loss (functional cure) for HBV
    **Typical Duration Months:**
      - Treatment Hcv: 2-3
      - Treatment Hbv: 12-48+
    **Design Notes:**
      - HCV: Non-inferiority to SOC DAA regimen, SVR12 near 95-100%
      - HBV: Functional cure (HBsAg loss) rare with current therapies; new endpoints emerging


## Gastroenterology Hepatology
**Inflammatory Bowel Disease:**
  **Ulcerative Colitis:**
    **Phase 3 Benchmarks:**
      **Typical Sample Size:**
        - Range: 500-1000
      **Primary Endpoints:**
        - Clinical remission: adapted Mayo score (stool frequency subscore ≤1, rectal bleeding subscore 0, endoscopic subscore ≤1)
      **Key Secondary:**
        - Endoscopic improvement (Mayo endoscopic subscore 0-1)
        - Mucosal healing
        - Histological remission (Geboes index)
        - Corticosteroid-free remission
      **Design Standards:**
        - Induction (8-12 weeks) + Maintenance (52 weeks) study design
        - Placebo-controlled for induction; re-randomisation or continuous treatment for maintenance
        - Central endoscopy reading mandatory
        - Moderate-to-severe UC population (adapted Mayo 6-12)
        - Biologic-experienced subgroup analysis
  **Crohns Disease:**
    **Phase 3 Benchmarks:**
      **Typical Sample Size:**
        - Range: 500-1200
      **Primary Endpoints:**
        - Clinical remission: CDAI <150 or PRO-2 remission (stool frequency ≤1.5/day, abdominal pain ≤1)
        - Endoscopic response: SES-CD reduction ≥50% from baseline
      **Key Secondary:**
        - Endoscopic remission (SES-CD ≤4)
        - Mucosal healing
        - Fistula closure (if applicable)
        - Steroid-free remission
      **Design Standards:**
        - PRO-based endpoints replacing CDAI per FDA guidance
        - Central endoscopy reading
        - Induction + maintenance design
        - Biologic-failure population: enrichment or stratification
**Nash Mash:**
  **Phase 3 Benchmarks:**
    **Typical Sample Size:**
      - Range: 1000-2500
    **Primary Endpoints:**
      - Resolution of steatohepatitis (NAS improvement) without worsening of fibrosis
      - Improvement in liver fibrosis by ≥1 stage without worsening of steatohepatitis
    **Secondary Endpoints:**
      - Liver-related clinical outcomes (decompensation, transplant, death)
      - Change in NAS components
      - Change in liver stiffness by elastography
    **Design Standards:**
      - Histological assessment via paired liver biopsy (baseline + end-of-treatment)
      - Phase III may use conditional approval pathway with histological endpoints, confirmatory study with clinical outcomes
      - FDA surrogate endpoint: fibrosis improvement or NASH resolution (accelerated approval basis)
      - Long-term outcome trials: 4-8+ years for liver-related clinical events
      - Central pathology reading mandatory


## Ophthalmology
**Retinal Diseases:**
  **Phase 3 Benchmarks:**
    **Typical Sample Size:**
      - Range: 300-800
    **Primary Endpoints:**
      - BCVA (Best Corrected Visual Acuity) change from baseline in ETDRS letters
      - Proportion gaining ≥15 letters BCVA
    **Secondary Endpoints:**
      - Central subfield thickness (CST) by OCT
      - Anatomic outcomes (retinal fluid)
      - Proportion with visual acuity ≥20/40
      - Treatment burden (number of injections)
    **Design Standards:**
      - ETDRS chart for visual acuity assessment (masked examiners)
      - OCT central reading centre
      - Active comparator (anti-VEGF) for wet AMD, DME
      - Sham injection control with mandatory rescue criteria for ethics
      - Non-inferiority margin: typically 4-5 ETDRS letters
      - Treatment interval comparison (e.g., treat-and-extend vs fixed dosing)
    **Therapeutic Areas:**
      - Wet Amd: Anti-VEGF comparator (aflibercept, ranibizumab); 48-week primary
      - Dme: Anti-VEGF comparator; stratify by baseline BCVA
      - Geographic Atrophy: Anatomic endpoint (GA lesion growth rate by FAF)


## Psychiatry
**Major Depressive Disorder:**
  **Phase 3 Benchmarks:**
    **Typical Sample Size:**
      - Range: 300-600
      - Note: High placebo response requires larger samples
    **Primary Endpoints:**
      - Change from baseline in MADRS or HAM-D total score at Week 6-8
    **Key Secondary:**
      - MADRS/HAM-D response (≥50% reduction)
      - MADRS/HAM-D remission (score ≤10 / ≤7)
      - CGI-S improvement
      - SDS (Sheehan Disability Scale) for function
    **Design Standards:**
      - Placebo-controlled (active comparator reference arm optional but recommended)
      - High placebo response rate (30-40%) — major design challenge
      - Strategies to reduce placebo response: single-blind placebo lead-in, centralised rating, SIGMA rater training
      - 6-8 week acute treatment phase
      - Suicidality monitoring: C-SSRS at every visit (FDA requirement)
      - Treatment-resistant depression (TRD): STAR*D-aligned staging
    **Regulatory Expectations:**
      - Fda: Two adequate well-controlled studies; suicidality boxed warning for <25 years; C-SSRS mandatory
      - Ema: Active comparator recommended; relapse prevention study for long-term efficacy
**Schizophrenia:**
  **Phase 3 Benchmarks:**
    **Typical Sample Size:**
      - Range: 300-500
    **Primary Endpoints:**
      - Change from baseline in PANSS total score at Week 4-6
    **Design Standards:**
      - Placebo-controlled, 4-6 week acute phase
      - PANSS and CGI-S as co-primary or key secondary
      - Long-acting injectable (LAI) studies: relapse prevention design (randomised withdrawal)
      - Metabolic monitoring: weight, glucose, lipids, prolactin
      - EPS monitoring: AIMS, Barnes, Simpson-Angus scales


## Gene Cell Therapy
**Gene Therapy:**
  **Phase 3 Benchmarks:**
    **Typical Sample Size:**
      - Range: 20-200
      - Note: Often rare diseases, small populations
    **Primary Endpoints:**
      - Disease-specific functional endpoint
      - Biomarker-based endpoint (e.g., factor VIII levels for haemophilia)
      - Time to event (e.g., vaso-occlusive crises for SCD)
    **Design Standards:**
      - Single-arm with historical/natural history comparator common
      - Intra-patient comparison (pre-treatment vs post-treatment)
      - Long-term follow-up: 15 years for integrating vectors (FDA), 5 years minimum for non-integrating
      - Dose selection based on non-clinical and Phase I/II data
      - Risk monitoring: insertional mutagenesis, immunogenicity, off-target effects
      - Manufacturing: vein-to-vein process, turnaround time, potency testing
      - Conditional/accelerated approval pathways commonly used
    **Regulatory Expectations:**
      - Fda: RMAT designation, BLA pathway, long-term follow-up per FDA gene therapy guidance
      - Ema: ATMP regulation, CAT assessment, conditional MA common, 15-year safety follow-up
    **Success Rates:**
      - Phase 3 Success: 40-55%
      - Note: Improving with better vector technology; manufacturing remains key challenge
**Car T Cell Therapy:**
  **Phase 3 Benchmarks:**
    **Typical Sample Size:**
      - Range: 100-300
    **Primary Endpoints:**
      - Overall response rate (ORR) for single-arm (accelerated approval)
      - Event-free survival (EFS) for randomised vs SOC
    **Design Standards:**
      - Single-arm with historical comparator or randomised vs SOC
      - CRS grading: ASTCT consensus scale
      - ICANS (neurotoxicity) grading and management protocol
      - Bridging therapy during manufacturing period
      - Manufacturing success rate reporting (% of patients receiving product)
      - Long-term follow-up: secondary malignancy monitoring (T-cell lymphoma — FDA class-wide boxed warning 2024)
      - REMS may be required for CRS/ICANS


## Vaccines
**Prophylactic Vaccines:**
  **Phase 3 Benchmarks:**
    **Typical Sample Size:**
      - Range: 3000-40000
      - Note: Large event-driven efficacy trials
    **Primary Endpoints:**
      - Vaccine efficacy (VE): 1 - (attack rate vaccinated / attack rate placebo)
      - Prevention of confirmed disease (PCR-confirmed or clinical criteria)
    **Secondary Endpoints:**
      - Prevention of severe disease
      - Prevention of hospitalisation/death
      - Immunogenicity: seroconversion rate, GMT (Geometric Mean Titre)
      - Duration of protection
    **Design Standards:**
      - Randomised, observer-blind, placebo-controlled (or active comparator for established vaccines)
      - Event-driven design with pre-specified VE threshold
      - FDA generally requires VE ≥50% (lower bound of 95% CI >30%)
      - Immunobridging: approved in some settings to extrapolate to new populations based on immune response
      - Lot-to-lot consistency studies for manufacturing
      - Co-administration studies with routine vaccines
      - Safety: large safety database (≥3000 vaccinated; FDA often requires larger), follow-up for rare adverse events
      - DSMB mandatory with pre-specified interim analyses
    **Regulatory Expectations:**
      - Fda: Pre-EUA/BLA meeting, FDA VRBPAC advisory committee review, VAERS post-marketing surveillance
      - Ema: CHMP assessment, conditional MA pathway available, PASS for long-term safety
      - Who: WHO prequalification for global access; WHO Target Product Profile alignment


## Renal Disease
**Chronic Kidney Disease:**
  **Phase 3 Benchmarks:**
    **Typical Sample Size:**
      - Range: 2000-6000
      - Note: Event-driven designs
    **Primary Endpoints:**
      - Composite: sustained ≥40-57% eGFR decline, ESKD (dialysis or transplant), or renal death
      - Time to kidney failure (ESKD)
    **Secondary Endpoints:**
      - eGFR slope (rate of decline over time)
      - Albuminuria reduction
      - CV events (many CKD trials include CV composite)
      - All-cause mortality
    **Design Standards:**
      - Event-driven design with adjudication committee
      - Enrichment for high-risk CKD (eGFR 25-60 mL/min, UACR >200 mg/g)
      - Active control or standard of care background therapy
      - eGFR slope as surrogate endpoint for accelerated approval per FDA guidance
      - Long follow-up: 2-4+ years for renal outcome events
      - FDA 2022 guidance: eGFR endpoints for accelerated approval in CKD


## Pain Analgesia
**Chronic Pain:**
  **Phase 3 Benchmarks:**
    **Typical Sample Size:**
      - Range: 300-800
    **Primary Endpoints:**
      - Change in NRS (Numerical Rating Scale) pain intensity from baseline
      - Proportion with ≥30% or ≥50% pain reduction (responder analysis)
    **Secondary Endpoints:**
      - Patient Global Impression of Change (PGIC)
      - BPI (Brief Pain Inventory)
      - Physical function
      - Sleep quality
      - Rescue medication use
    **Design Standards:**
      - Enriched enrollment randomised withdrawal (EERW) design accepted by FDA
      - Parallel group placebo-controlled design (EMA preference)
      - High placebo response: 30-40% (similar challenge to psychiatry)
      - Abuse-deterrent formulation assessment for opioids
      - Suicidality monitoring for chronic pain trials (C-SSRS)
      - FDA encourages non-opioid alternatives


## Quality Scoring Benchmarks
- Description: Scoring thresholds used by NIX AI for protocol quality assessment
**Overall Quality Tiers:**
  **Excellent:**
    - Score Range: 85-100
    - Description: Publication-ready protocol meeting all ICH/regional requirements with comprehensive endpoints and statistics
  **Good:**
    - Score Range: 70-84
    - Description: Solid protocol with minor gaps that can be addressed in amendments
  **Adequate:**
    - Score Range: 50-69
    - Description: Meets minimum requirements but has notable deficiencies
  **Needs Improvement:**
    - Score Range: 30-49
    - Description: Significant gaps in design, endpoints, or methodology
  **Major Revision Needed:**
    - Score Range: 0-29
    - Description: Fundamental issues requiring protocol redesign
**Component Weights:**
  - Study Design: 0.2
  - Endpoint Selection: 0.2
  - Statistical Methodology: 0.15
  - Patient Population: 0.1
  - Safety Monitoring: 0.1
  - Regulatory Compliance: 0.1
  - Hta Readiness: 0.1
  - Operational Feasibility: 0.05
