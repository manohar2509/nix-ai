## Citation Quality Rules
- Every finding must reference at least one authoritative source
- ICH guidelines take precedence as they are globally harmonised
- Regional guidance cited when jurisdiction-specific requirement
- HTA body manuals cited for payer-specific recommendations
- URLs must point to official government/organization sources only
- Citation includes: source code, title, specific section, relevance explanation
- Confidence score reflects strength of citation: ICH Step 4 = highest, Draft = lower
- Multiple citations strengthen findings — cross-reference ICH + regional when possible

### Authority Hierarchy
  1. ICH Step 4 Guidelines (globally accepted)
  2. National regulatory requirements (21 CFR, EU CTR, PMD Act)
  3. FDA/EMA Final Guidance Documents
  4. HTA Body Official Manuals (NICE, IQWiG, CADTH, PBAC)
  5. ICH Step 2-3 Draft Guidelines
  6. FDA/EMA Draft Guidance Documents
  7. Published clinical literature (PubMed/MEDLINE)
  8. Clinical trial registry data (ClinicalTrials.gov)
