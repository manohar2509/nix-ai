# ICH Harmonised Guidelines — Complete Reference

This document contains the complete ICH guideline database used by NIX AI
for clinical trial protocol analysis, compliance scoring, and citation.


## Efficacy Guidelines (E-Series)

### ICH E1: The Extent of Population Exposure to Assess Clinical Safety
- **URL**: https://database.ich.org/sites/default/files/E1_Guideline.pdf
- **Focus**: Safety database size requirements for marketing applications
- **Effective Date**: 1994-10-27

  **Compliance Criteria for ICH E1:**
  CRITICAL:
    - Minimum 100 patients exposed for 12 months for chronic indications
    - Total database ≥1500 patients
  MAJOR:
    - 300-600 patients with 6-month exposure
    - Special population representation
  MINOR:
    - Dose-response data availability

### ICH E2A: Clinical Safety Data Management: Definitions and Standards for Expedited Reporting
- **URL**: https://database.ich.org/sites/default/files/E2A_Guideline.pdf
- **Focus**: Definitions of adverse events, serious adverse events, and expedited reporting requirements
- **Effective Date**: 1994-10-27

  **Compliance Criteria for ICH E2A:**
  CRITICAL:
    - SAE definition aligned with ICH E2A
    - Expedited reporting timelines in protocol
    - DSMB/DMC charter for safety oversight
  MAJOR:
    - AE grading scale specified (CTCAE or equivalent)
    - Causality assessment methodology defined
  MINOR:
    - Adverse event collection period specified post-treatment

### ICH E2F: Development Safety Update Report
- **URL**: https://database.ich.org/sites/default/files/E2F_Guideline.pdf
- **Focus**: Annual safety reporting during clinical development
- **Effective Date**: 2010-08-17

  **Compliance Criteria for ICH E2F:**
  CRITICAL:
    - DSUR submission plan documented
    - Benefit-risk assessment framework defined
  MAJOR:
    - Reference safety information (RSI) specified
    - Signal detection methodology described
  MINOR:
    - DSUR submission timeline aligned with DIBD

### ICH E3: Structure and Content of Clinical Study Reports

  CRITICAL:

  MINOR:

### ICH E4: Dose-Response Information to Support Drug Registration
- **URL**: https://database.ich.org/sites/default/files/E4_Guideline.pdf
- **Focus**: Dose selection, dose-response studies, PK/PD relationships
- **Effective Date**: 1994-03-10

  **Compliance Criteria for ICH E4:**
  CRITICAL:
    - Dose selection rationale documented with PK/PD data or dose-response study
  MAJOR:

    - Dose adjustment for renal/hepatic impairment
  MINOR:

  MAJOR:

  MINOR:

### ICH E6_R3: Good Clinical Practice
- **URL**: https://database.ich.org/sites/default/files/ICH_E6%28R3%29_Step4_FinalGuideline_2025_0106_ErrorCorrections_2025_1024.pdf
- **Focus**: Trial conduct, quality, data integrity, informed consent, risk-based approaches
- **Effective Date**: 2025-01-06

  **Compliance Criteria for ICH E6_R3:**
  CRITICAL:
    - Informed consent process documented per Section 4.8
    - IRB/IEC approval obtained before enrollment
    - Risk-based quality management plan
    - Protocol includes all Section 6 elements
    - Investigator qualifications verified
    - Safety reporting procedures defined
  MAJOR:
    - Risk-based monitoring strategy documented (Section 5.18)
    - Data integrity measures specified
    - Critical to quality (CtQ) factors identified
    - Essential documents list maintained
    - Amendment handling procedures defined
  MINOR:
    - Trial Master File (TMF) management plan
    - Technology platforms for data capture specified

### ICH E9_R1: Statistical Principles for Clinical Trials — Addendum: Estimands and Sensitivity Analysis
- **URL**: https://database.ich.org/sites/default/files/E9-R1_Step4_Guideline_2019_1203.pdf
- **Focus**: Estimands framework, intercurrent events, sensitivity analysis strategies
- **Effective Date**: 2019-12-03

  **Compliance Criteria for ICH E9_R1:**
  CRITICAL:
    - Primary estimand explicitly defined with all 5 attributes
    - Intercurrent events identified with handling strategies
    - SAP describes sensitivity analyses aligned with estimand
  MAJOR:
    - Missing data strategy aligned with estimand
    - Supplementary estimands for secondary questions
    - Sample size justified for primary estimand
  MINOR:
    - Estimand framework training for study team documented

### ICH E10: Choice of Control Group and Related Issues in Clinical Trials
- **URL**: https://database.ich.org/sites/default/files/E10_Guideline.pdf
- **Focus**: Active vs. placebo controls, non-inferiority, assay sensitivity
- **Effective Date**: 2000-07-20

  **Compliance Criteria for ICH E10:**
  CRITICAL:
    - Control group selection justified scientifically and ethically
    - Non-inferiority margin justified with historical data if applicable
    - Assay sensitivity addressed for non-inferiority trials
  MAJOR:
    - Placebo use ethically justified per Declaration of Helsinki
    - Active comparator reflects standard of care
  MINOR:
    - Rescue medication criteria specified

    - Age-appropriate dose selection justified
    - Assent/consent procedures for each age group
  MAJOR:
    - Extrapolation framework justified if used

    - Long-term safety monitoring plan
  MINOR:
    - Age-appropriate formulation development plan

### ICH E14: The Clinical Evaluation of QT/QTc Interval Prolongation and Proarrhythmic Potential for Non-Antiarrhythmic Drugs

### ICH E20: Adaptive Clinical Trials
- **URL**: https://database.ich.org/sites/default/files/ICH_E20EWG_Step3_DraftGuideline_2025_0627.pdf
- **Focus**: Adaptive designs, interim analyses, type I error control, operational bias control
- **Effective Date**: 2025-06-27

  **Compliance Criteria for ICH E20:**
  CRITICAL:
    - All adaptations pre-specified in protocol and SAP
    - Type I error control demonstrated via simulation
    - DMC charter for interim analysis decision-making
  MAJOR:
    - Operating characteristics evaluated via simulation
    - Blinding maintained during adaptations where possible
    - Decision rules for each adaptation pre-specified
  MINOR:
    - Regulatory consultation for novel adaptive designs

## Safety Guidelines (S-Series)

### ICH S1A: Need for Carcinogenicity Studies of Pharmaceuticals
- **URL**: https://database.ich.org/sites/default/files/S1A_Guideline.pdf
- **Focus**: When carcinogenicity studies are required based on duration of use, genotoxicity signals, structural alerts

  **Compliance Criteria for ICH S1A:**
  CRITICAL:
    - Carcinogenicity study need assessment documented
    - Studies initiated before Phase III for drugs requiring them
  MAJOR:
    - Two-species study plan or justified alternative approach
    - Genotoxicity battery completed before carcinogenicity study design
  MINOR:
    - Carcinogenicity study timeline aligned with registration filing

### ICH S2_R1: Guidance on Genotoxicity Testing and Data Interpretation
- **URL**: https://database.ich.org/sites/default/files/S2%28R1%29_Guideline.pdf
- **Focus**: Standard genotoxicity battery: in vitro gene mutation, chromosomal damage, in vivo test

  **Compliance Criteria for ICH S2_R1:**
  CRITICAL:
    - Complete genotoxicity battery before human exposure
    - Positive signals addressed with mechanistic follow-up studies
  MAJOR:
    - Standard battery completed per ICH S2(R1)
    - Weight-of-evidence assessment for equivocal results
  MINOR:
    - Follow-up study rationale documented

### ICH S5_R3: Detection of Toxicity to Reproduction for Human Pharmaceuticals
- **URL**: https://database.ich.org/sites/default/files/S5-R3_Step4_Guideline_2020_0218.pdf
- **Focus**: Fertility, embryo-fetal development, pre/postnatal development studies
- **Effective Date**: 2020-02-18

  **Compliance Criteria for ICH S5_R3:**
  CRITICAL:
    - EFD completed before WOCBP enrollment
    - Contraception requirements specified in protocol
  MAJOR:
    - Fertility and pre/postnatal studies planned per development timeline
    - Exposure margins calculated vs. clinical dose
  MINOR:
    - Juvenile animal study timeline for pediatric indications

### ICH S6_R1: Preclinical Safety Evaluation of Biotechnology-Derived Pharmaceuticals
- **URL**: https://database.ich.org/sites/default/files/S6_R1_Guideline.pdf
- **Focus**: Biologics-specific nonclinical requirements: species selection, immunogenicity, reproductive toxicity

  **Compliance Criteria for ICH S6_R1:**
  CRITICAL:
    - Relevant species justified
    - Immunogenicity monitoring in tox studies
  MAJOR:
    - ADA impact on PK/PD and toxicity interpretation
    - Reproductive toxicity strategy documented
  MINOR:
    - Tissue cross-reactivity completed for mAbs

### ICH S7B: Nonclinical Evaluation of the Potential for Delayed Ventricular Repolarization (QT Interval Prolongation)
- **URL**: https://database.ich.org/sites/default/files/S7B_Guideline.pdf
- **Focus**: hERG assay, in vivo QT studies, integrated risk assessment with E14

  **Compliance Criteria for ICH S7B:**
  CRITICAL:
    - hERG assay completed before first human dose
    - QT risk assessment documented
  MAJOR:
    - In vivo QT study or justified waiver
    - Integrated risk assessment per S7B/E14 Q&A
  MINOR:
    - Dose-dependent QTc relationship characterized

### ICH S9: Nonclinical Evaluation for Anticancer Pharmaceuticals
- **URL**: https://database.ich.org/sites/default/files/S9_Guideline.pdf
- **Focus**: Reduced nonclinical requirements for life-threatening oncology indications

  **Compliance Criteria for ICH S9:**
  CRITICAL:
    - S9 applicability justified (advanced vs early stage cancer)
    - Minimum tox package for Phase I complete
  MAJOR:
    - EFD planning for Phase III aligned
    - Genotoxicity battery completed before late-stage trials
  MINOR:
    - Immunotoxicity assessment for immunomodulatory agents

### ICH S11: Nonclinical Safety Testing in Support of Development of Paediatric Pharmaceuticals
- **URL**: https://database.ich.org/sites/default/files/S11_Step4_Guideline_2020_1112.pdf
- **Focus**: Juvenile animal studies, timing relative to pediatric clinical trials
- **Effective Date**: 2020-11-12

  **Compliance Criteria for ICH S11:**
  CRITICAL:
    - JAS need assessment documented
    - JAS completed before pediatric dosing if required
  MAJOR:
    - Age-appropriate endpoints selected
    - Species and age at dosing start justified
  MINOR:
    - JAS waiver rationale documented if not conducted

### ICH S12: Nonclinical Biodistribution Considerations for Gene Therapy Products
- **URL**: https://database.ich.org/sites/default/files/ICH_S12_Step4_Guideline_2023_0530.pdf
- **Focus**: Gene therapy biodistribution study design and timing
- **Effective Date**: 2023-05-30

  **Compliance Criteria for ICH S12:**
  CRITICAL:
    - Biodistribution study completed before first human dose

  MAJOR:

  MINOR:
    - Long-term persistence follow-up plan
